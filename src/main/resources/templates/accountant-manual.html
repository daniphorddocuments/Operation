Getting Started

Welcome to the Mahanga Accountant Portal. This comprehensive guide will help you manage all financial operations and generate detailed reports.

1.1 Accessing Your Dashboard

Log in with your accountant credentials to access the accounting dashboard. You'll immediately see:

  • Financial summary for the current period
  • Outstanding balances and payments
  • Recent transactions
  • Pending reconciliations
  • Important alerts and reminders

1.2 Dashboard Organization

Your workspace includes:

  • Left Sidebar: Main navigation to all accounting functions
  • Top Bar: Quick access to reports, search, and notifications
  • Main Content Area: Detailed financial data and transaction management
  • Status Panel: Real-time financial metrics

1.3 Before You Start

Ensure you have:

  • Access credentials for your accountant account
  • Understanding of your organization's chart of accounts
  • List of active shops and their account codes
  • Current period information (month, fiscal year)
💼 Important: Always download or print reports for your records and audit trails.

Financial Dashboard

The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of your financial position at a glance.

2.1 Key Metrics

The dashboard displays the following important metrics:

Metric Description Use Case
Total Revenue Sum of all sales across all shops Monitor income generation
Total Expenses Sum of recorded operational expenses Control cost management
Net Profit Revenue minus expenses Measure profitability
Outstanding Payments Invoices awaiting payment Cash flow planning
Outstanding Loans Current loan balances Debt management

2.2 Dashboard Charts

Visual representations help you quickly identify trends:

  • Revenue Trend: Line chart showing revenue over time
  • Expense Breakdown: Pie chart of expense categories
  • Shop Comparison: Bar chart comparing shop performance
  • Monthly Performance: Combined chart of revenue vs expenses

2.3 Real-Time Updates

Dashboard metrics update automatically as:

  • Sales transactions are recorded
  • Expenses are entered
  • Payments are processed
  • Reconciliations are completed
📊 Tip: Refresh your browser periodically to ensure you're viewing the latest data.

Financial Reports

Generate comprehensive financial reports for analysis, compliance, and decision-making.

3.1 Revenue Reports

Track all incoming revenue:

  • 1Navigate to Reports → Revenue
  • 2Select date range
  • 3Choose breakdown level (by shop, product, etc.)
  • 4Apply filters as needed
  • 5Generate and review report
  • 6Export or print for records

3.2 Expense Reports

Analyze operational expenses:

  • View expenses by category
  • Compare budgeted vs actual expenses
  • Identify cost-saving opportunities
  • Track expense trends over time

3.3 Profit & Loss Statement (P&L)

The comprehensive P&L statement shows:

  • Total revenue for period
  • Cost of goods sold (COGS)
  • Operating expenses
  • Net profit or loss
  • Profit margin percentage

3.4 Cash Flow Report

Monitor cash movements:

  • Cash inflows from sales
  • Cash outflows for expenses
  • Net cash position
  • Cash balance projections

3.5 Invoice Report

Track invoicing and receivables:

  • All issued invoices
  • Payment status
  • Outstanding amounts
  • Aging analysis
📈 Best Practice: Generate P&L reports monthly for management review and planning.

Payment Processing

Record and manage all financial transactions and payments.

4.1 Recording Payments

To record a payment:

  • 1Click on "Record Payment"
  • 2Select payment type (Fee, Loan, Expense, etc.)
  • 3Select recipient/shop
  • 4Enter amount
  • 5Choose payment method
  • 6Add reference number (optional)
  • 7Enter notes/description
  • 8Confirm and save

4.2 Payment Methods

Supported payment methods include:

  • Cash transactions
  • Mobile money (M-Pesa, Airtel Money, etc.)
  • Bank transfers
  • Checks
  • Credit/Debit cards

4.3 Payment Reconciliation

Reconcile payments regularly:

  • Match recorded payments with bank statements
  • Investigate discrepancies
  • Mark reconciled transactions
  • Generate reconciliation reports
⚠️ Important: Always verify payment details before confirming to prevent errors.

Loan Management

Track and manage shop loans and financing.

5.1 Viewing Active Loans

Access all loan information:

  • Navigate to Loans section
  • View list of all active and inactive loans
  • See loan details: amount, term, interest rate
  • Check repayment status

5.2 Loan Components

Each loan record includes:

  • Principal Amount: Original loan amount
  • Interest Rate: Annual percentage rate
  • Repayment Term: Duration of loan
  • Monthly Payment: Expected payment amount
  • Outstanding Balance: Current owed amount
  • Status: Active or paid off

5.3 Recording Loan Payments

Process loan repayments:

  • Go to the specific loan
  • Click "Record Payment"
  • Enter payment amount
  • Add payment date
  • Select payment method
  • Save and confirm

5.4 Loan Reports

Generate loan-related reports:

  • Loan summary by shop
  • Repayment schedules
  • Overdue payment alerts
  • Loan portfolio analysis
📋 Reminder: Review loan repayment schedules monthly and follow up on overdue payments promptly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I reconcile my bank account?

A: In the Reconciliation section, select your bank account, download the bank statement, and match transactions with recorded payments. Mark each transaction as reconciled.

Q: What should I do if a payment is recorded incorrectly?

A: Contact your administrator to reverse the transaction. Maintain a record of all corrections for audit purposes.

Q: How often should I generate financial reports?

A: Daily for cash position monitoring, weekly for detailed analysis, and monthly for comprehensive reporting and management review.

Q: Can I export reports in different formats?

A: Yes, reports can be exported as PDF, Excel, or CSV depending on your needs.

Q: How do I handle multi-currency transactions?

A: All transactions are recorded in the system's base currency (typically TZS). Exchange rates are applied automatically at transaction time.

Q: What is the audit trail?

A: Every transaction is logged with timestamp, user information, and changes made. Access audit logs from the Audit section.

Q: How do I monitor cash flow?

A: Use the Cash Flow Report to see all inflows and outflows. Create projections based on historical data and planned transactions.
